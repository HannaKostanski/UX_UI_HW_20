Toilet Finder App

Case Study for an academic group project

Overview

Project Name:
Toilet Finder App (temporarily called “iPoo”)

Project Description:
Using reliable reviews, search filters, a map option, and other features, we hope to provide a network of desirable bathrooms in a user’s immediate area.

Team Members:
Hanna Kostanski, Ruhab Ilyas, Tracy Wong

My Role:
UX/UI Designer, Content Writer

Tools Used:
Slack, Google Meet, Miro, Google Slides, Zoom, Figma

The Problem

People face challenges finding and selecting appropriate public washrooms in a timely manner because public bathrooms vary in cleanliness, reliability, accessibility, and safety.

The Solution

Using interviews, research of other apps, and brainstorming, we have developed an app that can offer peace of mind for people who are anxious and private about their bathroom habits.

Reseach Plan

We planned and conducted five 1-to-1 interviews with potential users age 13 to 70 and a quantitative survey was created using Google Forms. The collection of data from the interviews and survey will allow our team gain a deeper insight into our customer thoughts and feelings. We conducted the following tools: Affinity Map, User Persona and Storyboard.

Competitor Analysis

We conducted a competitor analysis for three apps, namely Toilet Time, Easy PZ Toilet Finder, and Flush Toilet Finder. This analysis was essential to understand the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (SWOT) of these competitors. By evaluating their features, user experience, marketing strategies, and market positioning, we gained valuable insights into what works well and where improvements can be made. This information is helping us refine our own app's offering, differentiate it from competitors, and capitalize on untapped market opportunities.

User Insight Statement

Based on our research, we discovered that people need reliable public bathrooms. The stress of finding a clean, reliable and safe public bathroom can turn into a negative incident and that we might be able to help if we can provide a easier solution to help navigate our users to the closest preferred public bathroom. We might do this by creating a smarter way to filter public bathrooms based on personal preference and reliable recommendations from other users. Doing this will allow our product to help users to can offer peace of mind for people who are anxious and private about their bathroom habits.

Key Takeaways

  • I’m particularly happy with our final prototype’s usability, features, and the concept itself. I like how simple the idea is, and proud of the features we incorporated.

  • Working with a group in an academic setting comes with some necessary compromises, concessions, and a fairly tight timeline. While the concept is strong, moving forward, I would like to pivot towards an app that is somewhat more discreet and mature - further testing would be needed to determine the direction.

    I would like to iterate a more polished version, keeping in mind:

    • Changing the name “iPoo” to something more modest
    • While the dog mascot is very cute, it’s not my personal taste, and it reads a little juvenile; I’d like to keep some of the feeling of delight but in a more polished, mature package
    • Adding a “Report image” function
